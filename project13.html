Functionality

In my latest project, I ventured into the complex world of creating an Entity-Relationship (ER) diagram for a Hospital environment. The ER diagram was meticulously crafted to capture all the relevant entities, attributes, and relationships that exist within this domain. I also provided thorough explanations for each of the design choices I made to ensure that the final diagram was not only accurate but also intuitive and easy to understand.


Once the ER diagram was finalized, I proceeded to create 3NF relations based on the diagram. This involved breaking down the relationships into their respective components and ensuring that each table had a unique primary key. This step was crucial in optimizing the database's performance and reducing redundancy.


Finally, I implemented the design by creating several Database tables using Postgres. These tables were crafted in a way that aligned with the ER diagram and 3NF relations to ensure that the database was robust, efficient, and easy to maintain. Overall, this project was a challenging but rewarding endeavor that allowed me to delve into the intricacies of database design and implementation.
